About

Welcome. I am a queer Black scholar-practitioner, first-generation college graduate, educator, and photographer. I work in Student Affairs at the University of Pittsburgh, where I focus on first-year college transitions and the out-of-classroom experiences that stay with students long after they graduate. My doctoral research in Higher Education uses visual ethnography to center the voices of queer and trans students navigating the early moments of college life.

seen&centered is where my scholarship, photography, and practice live together. Through still photography I create images that hold space for identity, intimacy, and becoming. Through my research I explore how queer students find belonging in higher education and what it means to arrive somewhere and to stay yourself.

This work is still becoming. But it is rooted in a deep commitment to the people, stories, and lives that made it possible.

Seeing people. Centering stories.

Research

Milestone 1 · In Progress

More Than the First Year: Queer Students Narrating Belonging Across Early College Transition Through Photovoice

This study explores how queer first and second year students narrate and make meaning of belonging across their early college experience. Using Photovoice as a participatory research method, the study centers queer students as visual and narrative storytellers, moving beyond first-year transition frameworks to examine how belonging unfolds, shifts, and is contested over time. Grounded in Queer Theory and sense of belonging scholarship, this research attends to both the relational and structural conditions that shape whether belonging for queer students is genuinely possible. Advised by Dr. Maximilian T. Schuster, University of Pittsburgh.
