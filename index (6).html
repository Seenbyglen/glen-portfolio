About

Welcome. I am a queer Black scholar-practitioner, first-generation college graduate, educator, and photographer. I work in Student Affairs at the University of Pittsburgh, where I focus on first-year college transitions and the out-of-classroom experiences that stay with students long after they graduate. My doctoral research in Higher Education uses visual ethnography to center the voices of queer and trans students navigating the early moments of college life.

seen&centered is where my scholarship, photography, and practice live together. Through still photography I create images that hold space for identity, intimacy, and becoming. Through my research I explore how queer students find belonging in higher education and what it means to arrive somewhere and to stay yourself.

This work is still becoming. But it is rooted in a deep commitment to the people, stories, and lives that made it possible.

Seeing people. Centering stories.

Research

Academic work

Dissertation · In progress

Queer Students' Sense of Belonging in Early College Transition

A qualitative study drawing on photovoice methodology to understand how queer students construct and navigate belonging during the critical first year of college. Dissertation proposal submitted fall 2026.

Methodology

Photovoice as a Method for Centering Student Voice

Exploring the use of participant-generated photography as a tool for surfacing lived experience in higher education research, particularly with populations whose narratives are historically underrepresented.

Professional practice

New Student Programs & Outside the Classroom Curriculum

Applied work in student affairs examining how structured out-of-classroom experiences shape early belonging and identity development for new students.

Theoretical framework

Community Cultural Wealth & Queer Belonging

Applying Yosso's Community Cultural Wealth framework alongside queer theory to challenge deficit-oriented narratives in higher education research on LGBTQ+ students.
